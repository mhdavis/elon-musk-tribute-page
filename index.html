ELON MUSK

MODERN DAY INNOVATOR

Elon Musk giving a talk on the most recent work of SpaceX, one of the many companies he has founded.

TIMELINE

  • 1971 - Born in Pretoria, Transvaal South Africa
  • 1983 - Sold the BASIC-based video game code Blastar to the magazine PC and Office Technology for $500
  • 1990 - Attended Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario
  • 1992 - Transferred to University of Pennsylvania, where he received a B.S. in Physics and B.S. in Economics
  • 1995 - Moved to California to begin a PhD in Applied Physics and Material Sciences at Stanford University, but left the program to pursue entrepreneurial aspirations; developed Zip2 with his brother Kimbal.
  • 1999 - Zip2 sold for $307 million in cash and $34 in stock options, of which Musk recieved 7% (or $22 million dollars.) Musk used approximately half to start X.com
  • 2000 - Musk is ousted from his role as CEO of PayPal
  • 2001 - X.com acquires PayPal after merging with Confinity.
  • 2002 - eBay acquires PayPal, for which Musk is the company's largest shareholder (11.7%). Musk uses his earnings to launch SpaceX
  • 2004 - Musk joins in funding collection for Tesla Motors
  • 2008 - Financial crash occurs; Musk assumes leadership of Tesla Motors and the company releases the Tesla Roadster
  • 2012 - Musk announces collaberation between Tesla Motors and SolarCity (a company for which he provided intial funding and is a major stockholder)
  • 2013 - Musk unveils concept for Hyperloop, a high-speed transportation systems which functions via reduced pressure tubes.
  • 2015 - Musk announces creation of OpenAI, a not-for-profit artifical intelligence research company aimed at developing artificial general intelligence in a safe and beneficial way for human, while making AI accessible for public use.

LAUNCHED COMPANIES

PAYPAL

PayPal (1991-2001) was founded and shaped by Musk along with his colleagues Peter Thiel and Reid Hoffman (who would letter go on to pursue entreperneurial projects of their own.) The PayPal facilitated monetary transactions between independent businesses and their customers online, allowing all independent vendors to dictate their own prices.

SPACEX

After PayPal, Musk founded SpaceX (2001) with the hope of making humankind a spacefairing and multiplanetary species as its ultimate goal. The company's immediate goals are to lower the cost of rocket fabrication, including making rockets reusable for multiple missions.

TESLA MOTORS

Tesla Motors (2003) was founded by Martin Ebenhard and Marc Tarpenning, with Musk contributing monetarily to the company upon launch and sitting as its chairman beginning in 2004. In 2008, Musk took a more active role in the company, becoming its CEO. The vision of Tesla as stated by Musk is to jumpstart advancement in the electric car industry in an effort to combat the effects of climate change.
